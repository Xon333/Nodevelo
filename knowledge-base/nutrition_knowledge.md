# Nutrition Knowledge

How to phrase fueling guidance in workout descriptions. The NUMBERS (daily kcal
target, pre/in/post-ride carb grams, protein grams) are always pre-computed by
the app's deterministic formula and supplied with each generation request —
copy them exactly, never recalculate.

## Principles behind the formula

- For cycling, work in kJ ≈ kcal burned (1:1) — gross efficiency (~22–24%) and the kcal-to-kJ conversion roughly cancel out.
- Training days: base calories + session kJ + buffer. Rest days: flat target.
- The buffer auto-adjusts on the 7-day weight trend so weight change stays gradual (±0.3 kg/week band).

## Pre-ride (use the supplied grams)

- Timing: main carb meal 2–3 h before; top up 30 g 30 min before hard sessions.
- Good sources: oats, rice, bread/toast with jam, bananas; low fibre and low fat before intensity.
- Morning sessions: a smaller meal 60–90 min before is acceptable; shift the remainder to the previous evening.

## In-ride (use the supplied g/hr)

- Only fuel rides over 60 minutes.
- Sources by rate: up to ~60 g/hr glucose/maltodextrin alone is fine; above 60 g/hr use a 2:1 glucose:fructose mix (drink mix, gels, gummies) to use both gut transporters.
- Real-food options for Z2 rides: bananas (~25 g), rice cakes, dried fruit, jam sandwiches.
- Start eating within the first 20 minutes; aim for even 15–20 min spacing.
- High targets (90+ g/hr) need gut training — mention "practice race fueling" in hard long sessions.

## Post-ride (use the supplied grams)

- Window: within 60 minutes, then a normal meal 2–3 h later.
- Carbs: supplied target (≈1.0–1.2 g/kg). Protein: supplied target (25–35 g, e.g. Greek yogurt, milk, whey, eggs).
- After evening sessions, prioritise the recovery shake/meal even close to bedtime.

## Daily targets

- Quote the supplied "Daily target" verbatim in every description.
- On rest days emphasise protein distribution (4 × 25–35 g across the day) and slightly lower carbohydrate, higher protein/fat composition.

## Hydration

- 500–750 ml/h moderate conditions; add sodium (300–600 mg/L) for sessions over 90 min or hot days.
