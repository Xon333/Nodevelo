# Training Knowledge

Principles for constructing training blocks. These guide every generated plan.

## Block structure

- A 4-week block is 3 loading weeks + 1 recovery week (volume −30–40%, keep 1 short intensity touch to stay sharp).
- A 2-week block is 2 loading weeks; assume recovery is handled by the next block's start or schedule a lighter final 2–3 days.
- One block = one primary emphasis (e.g. FTP development). Everything else is maintenance dosage.
- Progress one variable at a time: volume OR interval time-at-intensity, not both in the same week.
- Weekly load progression within a block: roughly +5–10%, never more than +15%.

## Intensity distribution

- Default to pyramidal/polarized: ~80% of time easy (Z1–Z2), ~20% moderate-hard.
- Maximum 2 high-intensity (Threshold/VO2max/SIT) sessions per week at amateur training volumes; 3 only in short, deliberate overload weeks for experienced athletes.
- Never schedule two high-intensity days back-to-back. Minimum pattern: hard / easy / hard.
- Long Z2 rides go on the days with most available time (typically weekends).

## Using fitness metrics (CTL / ATL / TSB)

- TSB below −20 at block start: begin with 2–3 easier days before the first quality session.
- TSB above +15: the athlete is fresh; the block can start loading immediately.
- Target CTL ramp of +3 to +6 per week during loading; flat-to-negative during recovery weeks.
- If recent average RPE is trending up while power trends down, bias the first week easier.

## Session ordering within a week

1. Hardest/most specific session when freshest (typically after the rest day).
2. Long endurance ride on the weekend.
3. Recovery or rest the day after the hardest session.
4. Strength after bike intervals on the same day, or on easy days — never the day before key intervals.

## Recovery rules

- At least 1 full rest day per week, 2 in recovery weeks.
- A recovery week still contains riding — short Z2 and one brief intensity touch (e.g. 3x3m at 95%) to maintain feel.
- Poor sleep trend (<7 h average) or rapidly falling weight: reduce planned intensity volume ~20% and flag easier alternatives in session descriptions.

## Durability (fatigue resistance)

- Late-block emphasis: move some interval work to the back half of long rides.
- Build "kJ before quality" gradually: 800 kJ → 1200 kJ → 1500 kJ of Z2 work before end-of-ride efforts.
