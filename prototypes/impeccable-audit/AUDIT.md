## Qualitative "critique/polish" pass (the LLM layer, beyond the detectors)
Detectors catch the deterministic tells; impeccable's `critique`/`polish` commands add a judgment pass.
A sample, on **`components/SyncNotice.tsx`** (refinement within our language, not a redesign):
- The error and warning banners share one tone ramp; the **error** could earn a touch more weight —
  a 2px left accent bar in `red-500` rather than just a tinted fill — to read as "blocking" vs the
  amber "FYI". Matches our existing left-stripe vocabulary (workout-type accents).
- The dismiss "✕" and the inline "Dismiss" link are redundant; keep the corner ✕ (standard) and drop
  the text link to reduce the action row to **Retry** + ✕ — less visual competition.
- `backdrop-blur` + `/95` on a fixed banner is good; add a subtle `dark:shadow-[0_0_24px_-10px_...]`
  glow in the accent hue to tie it to the cyberpunk dark language (consistent with hero cards).
- Copy: "Sync problem" → "Couldn't sync" reads more human (a ux-writing-skill-style nudge).

## Bottom line
On a tree we already refined twice, a *deterministic, DESIGN.md-aware* pass still found **~4 genuine
fixes** (palette drift, a dark-mode gap, an em-dash) plus a **39-instance consistency project**
(title= → InfoDot) and forced an explicit **density decision** (99 sub-12px) — all without an API call
and without touching app code. That's the case for adopting the *approach* (rules + DESIGN.md), and for
lifting these rules into our own repo rather than installing the third-party tool.
